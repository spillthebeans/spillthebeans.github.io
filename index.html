FAQ

  • How do I know I am addicted to caffeine?
    • To diagnose caffeine addiction, look for symptoms like difficulty sleeping, restlessness, nervousness, muscle twitching, increased heart rate, or stomach upset.

  • When are good times to drink coffee throughout the day?
    • Our biological clocks control a hormone in our bodies called cortisol, which makes us feel more awake. Since our biological clocks and cortisol production are mostly controlled by sunlight, the optimal time to drink coffee is around the same for everyone. So, optimal times for caffeine intake are between 9:30-11:30 a.m. and 1:30-5:30 p.m.

  • What happens if we consume caffeine during times that our cortisol production is high?
    • Two things will happen. The effects of the caffeine will be lessened, and our bodies will gain a high tolerance to caffeine.

  • Can caffeine addiction lead to more severe disorders?
    • Caffeine can induce other disorders like caffeine-induced anxiety disorder and caffeine-induced sleep disorder.

  • How long does caffeine stay in the body?
    • Caffeine reaches its peak level in the bloodstream in about 30 minutes to an hour. Then the body will get rid of roughly 50% of the caffeine in 3 to 5 hours and the rest can stay in the body for 8 to 14 hours.

  • Are energy drinks a good substitute for coffee?
    • Energy drinks contain significantly less caffeine than a single cup of coffee and instead have lots more sugar and chemicals. So, energy drinks will simply provide more sugar and empty calories for a smaller energy boost.

  • Can caffeine have positive effects on you?
    • If taken in the right amounts and at the proper times, caffeine can increase sharpness and concentration, and it has even been shown to increase lifespan.

  • Is it a good idea to drink coffee right before a nap?
    • Drinking a cup of coffee right before a short 15 minute nap will increase focus and concentration.

About

Our vision is to initiate a resource for college students to track the amount of caffeine they consume and help them reduce unhealthy use of the stimulant.

We hope to provide a website that can achieve this by reminding students of the harmful effects of a dependence on the stimulant, and providing a useful resource to log intake to calculate the approximate halflife of caffeine in their system.
